CAUSES OF FIRE



  • filter_dramaAccidents and Carelessness

    Many people believe that if they are careful they are much less likely to have a fire.

    While it is true that being careful will make you safer it will not stop fire from happening.

    Most fires are not caused from carelessness.

    They are caused from every day living that is almost impossible for us to change.


  • placeElectrical Wiring, Electrical Outlets and Faulty Wiring

    Whether it’s in an electrical outlet or a short in the wall, many fires are caused by electrical wiring.

    Older homes are particularly susceptible, as they were not wired for the many, many appliances that we have filled our homes with.

    Many homes that were built in the 50′s -70′s have aluminum wiring that gets very hot and increases the chance of fire.


  • whatshotAppliances

    Lamps, toasters and even baby monitors can short out.

    Be particularly careful with older appliances and extension cords.

    Even new appliances can be the source of a home fire.

    o be safe, appliances should be unplugged when not in use.

    Unfortunately, not all appliances can be unplugged, leaving your home at risk 24 hours a day.


  • insert_chartHeating

    Heating is another major cause of residential fire deaths

    This is especially true in southeastern states and among wood stove users in the north.


  • accessibilityUnattended Stoves

    Another cause of residential fires is cooking, but not due to defective stoves or ovens.

    Often, it is because of unattended pots or the burner being left on accidentally


  • albumChildren Playing with Matches

    Children and grandchildren playing with matches are a major source of home fires.
